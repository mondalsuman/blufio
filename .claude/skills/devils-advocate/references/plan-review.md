# Plan & Strategy Review — Devil's Advocate Checklist

## Assumptions to Challenge

### Market & Users
- Who exactly is the user? Have you talked to them?
- What if nobody wants this? What's the evidence of demand?
- What's the switching cost FROM competitors? Is it high enough to matter?
- Are you solving a real problem or a hypothetical one?
- What if the market is smaller than you think?

### Technical Feasibility
- Has this been done before? If yes, why did it fail? If no, why not?
- What's the hardest unsolved technical problem in this plan?
- What dependencies are you trusting that you don't control?
- What's the plan when a critical third-party API changes or dies?
- How long will this ACTUALLY take? (Multiply your estimate by 3.)

### Business Model
- Who pays? How much? Why that amount?
- What's the unit economics? Does it work at scale?
- What happens if your biggest customer leaves?
- What's the competitive moat? How fast can someone copy this?
- Legal/compliance risks? (GDPR, licensing, IP, regulations)

### Execution
- What's the MVP? Is it actually minimal or is it a bloated v1?
- What are the first 5 minutes like for a new user?
- What's the rollback plan if this fails?
- Who owns what? Are responsibilities clear?
- What are you NOT doing? Is the scope actually bounded?

### Hidden Risks
- What's the worst-case scenario you haven't discussed?
- What external event could kill this overnight? (regulation, platform change, competitor move)
- What happens if a key person leaves?
- Are there ethical concerns nobody's raising?
- What's the opportunity cost of doing THIS instead of something else?

## Output Format

Structure the review as:

1. **Summary Verdict** — One paragraph: overall assessment, biggest concern
2. **Assumptions Challenged** — List each assumption with why it might be wrong
3. **Risk Matrix** — Top 5 risks ranked by (likelihood × impact)
4. **Missing from the Plan** — What's not addressed that should be
5. **Stress Test Scenarios** — 3 concrete "what if" scenarios played out
6. **Grudging Praise** — What's actually good (be honest, it means more)
